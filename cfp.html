Call for Papers

Submission Guidelines

The CoDAIM 2026 workshop seeks research papers and position articles on innovations at the intersection of AI, systems, and architecture. We invite short (up to 2-page) submissions that present early-stage ideas, empirical results, or visionary perspectives.

Accepted papers will be presented as 15-minute talks and included in the workshop proceedings.

Paper Structure

Papers should include the following sections:

  1. Problem statement and motivating evidence
  2. State-of-the-art
  3. Proposed solution
  4. Quantitative evidence showing why the solution works

Optionally, authors can add a discussion/future work section. All content should fit within 2 pages. The paper can also have an unlimited number of references, and an optional section explaining how the authors used AI tools.

Topics of Interest

Topics include, but are not limited to:

  • Systems for agentic and multimodal AI
  • Foundations of generative and agentic inference
  • Cross-layer co-design: algorithm–compiler–runtime–architecture
  • Cost-, latency-, and energy-optimized inference
  • Hardware/software co-optimization for LLMs and multimodal models
  • Efficient model serving: batching, caching, speculative decoding
  • Memory, I/O, and scheduling optimizations in AI inference
  • Model compression and deployment strategies
  • AI-driven system optimization and auto-tuning
  • Evaluation methodologies and performance analysis for AI inference
  • Real-world case studies and workload characterization

We welcome both academic and industry submissions.

Review Process

Each paper will be reviewed by the program committee, consisting of faculty and senior industry researchers. To foster mentorship, PC members may nominate one student reviewer whose contributions will be formally recognized.

AI Use Policy

Authors may leverage AI tools for tasks such as writing assistance, code debugging, plotting, or grammar polishing. However, AI tools should not be used to autonomously generate full papers.

Each submission must include a brief statement (not counted toward the page limit) describing how AI tools were used.
